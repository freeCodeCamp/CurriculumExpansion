Ohm's Law

Ohm's Law is a fundamental principle in electrical engineering, stating that the current through a conductor between two points is directly proportional to the voltage across the two points.

This law is essential for understanding how electrical circuits operate.

I = V/R

Ohm's Law is used extensively in circuit analysis and design.

Circuit Analysis

Circuit analysis is the process of finding the currents through, and voltages across, every component in an electrical circuit.

This process is fundamental to designing any electrical system.

AC vs DC

Alternating Current (AC) and Direct Current (DC) are the two types of electrical current used in modern electrical systems.

AC is commonly used in power distribution, while DC is used in battery-powered devices.

Understanding the differences between AC and DC is crucial for any electrical engineer.
